Differentiable Point Resampling and Ray Transformation
Aid to Editable NeRF Models

Anonymous

Abstract

In NeRF-aided editing tasks, the removal operations of certain scene elements often lead to empty regions, presenting challenges for NeRF models in inpainting these scenes. Moreover, the movement of objects in practical applications presents significant difficulties in supervised learning for these models, as it introduces variability in object positions. To tackle these issues, we introduce a plug-and-play model, dubbed differentiable neural ray resampling (DNR), which interpolates occurring empty regions at the original ray locations within the implicit space, facilitating object removal & scene inpainting tasks. Importantly, employing DNR effectively bridges the gap between ground truth and predicted implicit feature distances, potentially increasing the mutual information of implicit features across rays. We propose a point-based editable NeRF pipeline, which leverages an implicit ray transformation for manipulating scene objects, and DNR enables effective inpainting of empty regions induced by transformations in various operational scenarios. Results primarily evaluated on 3D object removal & inpainting tasks indicate that our model achieves state-of-the-art performance. In addition, our pipeline offers versatility by supporting high-quality rendering visualization for diverse editing operations without requiring extra supervision.
